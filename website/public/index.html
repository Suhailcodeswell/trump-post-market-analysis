Does what Trump says affect financial markets?

Over two decades, Donald Trump has been one of the most watched figures in global finance — not as a trader, but as a source of sudden, market-moving commentary.

From Twitter posts about Bitcoin to warnings on Iran and tariff announcements, his words often arrive in real time while markets are open. The question is not whether people react — it is whether there is a repeatable pattern worth studying, and whether that pattern can be understood well enough to act on.

This analysis focuses on Trump's posting patterns and how they align with moves in Bitcoin, crude oil, the S&P 500, and the Nasdaq. It asks: does he move these markets, by how much, and over what horizon — and is there any practical way to capitalize on that timing?

Important: this is an association study, not proof of causation. Trump is rarely the only factor — especially for oil and broad equities. Results are for research and portfolio demonstration, not financial advice.

Market 01

Bitcoin

Bitcoin is the largest cryptocurrency by market cap — volatile, globally traded, and increasingly tied to policy rhetoric around digital assets. Trump's relationship with crypto has shifted dramatically: from calling it "based on thin air" in 2019 to pledging a U.S. crypto reserve in 2025.

We identified 28 Bitcoin-related posting events in the dataset. On average, Bitcoin does not reliably follow Trump's tone — macro cycles, ETF flows, and halving events dominate. Trump is a small factor in the long run, but individual posts can coincide with sharp short-term moves.

Context: Bitcoin's full price history from 2009 through 2025. The asset spent years near zero before explosive growth — any event study must be read against this backdrop, not in isolation.

Positive case — Oct 31, 2024: Trump posted about Bitcoin's anniversary and ending the "war on crypto." One month later, Bitcoin was up +37.4% on a $100k hypothetical entry — one of the strongest forward returns in the dataset. Still, the 2024–25 bull market was already underway.

Negative case — Jul 12, 2019: "I am not a fan of Bitcoin…" Bitcoin fell −10.9% within one week. This is often cited as evidence Trump moves crypto — but 2019 was also a period of regulatory uncertainty independent of any single tweet.

Outlier — Mar 2, 2025: A U.S. Crypto Reserve announcement. Despite positive sentiment, Bitcoin dropped sharply in the following week (−14.5%) — a reminder that "good news" posts do not always produce immediate gains. Markets often price policy before the post lands.

In office vs. not: Average one-week returns after Bitcoin posts differ by presidency era. Being in office does not automatically mean stronger moves — context and market regime matter more than the title on the post.

Source: Power BI report — event study on 73,380 cleaned posts (2009–2025).

Market 02

Oil

Crude oil is among the most geopolitically sensitive commodities on earth. War, OPEC supply decisions, inventory data, and global demand shifts routinely move prices by more than any social media post ever could.

Trump posts about Iran and energy 705 times in our tagged dataset — yet the average one-week return is slightly negative. Trump is an insignificant factor in the aggregate; we study patterns anyway because timing around crises (e.g. June 2025 Iran diplomacy) can still be informative.

Price context (2024–2025): Oil traded between roughly $55 and $90 per barrel — spikes in early 2025 aligned with Middle East tension, not posts alone.

June 2025 — Iran cluster: Each bar is one Trump post and the oil return one week later. Jun 12's diplomacy post preceded a +10.1% weekly move — but the same month included military headlines and supply shocks. Conditional coloring highlights magnitude, not causation.

Oil moves for many reasons. Treat post-event returns as timing snapshots, not trade signals.

Market 03

S&P 500

The S&P 500 tracks roughly 500 large U.S. companies — the broadest measure of American equity health. Trump posts about tariffs, China, and the economy more than any other topic: 1,772 one-day events in our study.

On average, the index drifts slightly higher after his posts (+0.3% at one week, +1.5% at one month). That sounds bullish — but much of 2009–2025 was a secular bull market. Trump is one voice among thousands of daily inputs.

Daily close (2024–2025): The index ranged from about 5,000 to nearly 6,900 points — including a sharp April 2025 drawdown and recovery tied to tariff headlines and broader macro fear.

Apr 8, 2025 — auto tariffs: A post on steering jobs back to the U.S. preceded an +8.3% one-week rally. Whether the post caused the move or simply coincided with a relief rally after prior tariff fear is debatable — the chart shows price before and after the event date.

Market 04

Nasdaq

The Nasdaq Composite is tech-heavy — Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, and hundreds of growth names. Trump's posts about Big Tech regulation and election-eve rhetoric map cleanly to this index.

Of 74 Nasdaq-tagged events, the one-month win rate is 79% — the highest in our study. Average one-month return: +3.0%. Again, tech's long-run outperformance explains much of this; Trump is not the primary driver.

Index context: Nasdaq performance alongside summary statistics from the event study — average returns, win rates, and event counts.

Nov 2, 2020 — election eve: A post attacking Biden and Big Tech preceded a +6.9% one-week Nasdaq gain. The post landed hours before a major election — one of the noisiest weeks in market history. Association, not attribution.

Documentation

About this project

A full-stack data analysis portfolio piece: raw social media data through Python, SQL modeling, Power BI visualization, and this Vercel-hosted website.

Data sources

  • Trump posts: 90,343 raw records → 73,380 cleaned posts (2009–2025) — deleted posts, pure reposts, and empty text removed
  • Market prices: Yahoo Finance daily OHLC — S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow, VIX, Bitcoin, WTI crude oil, gold
  • Event definitions: keyword topic tagging (Bitcoin, oil/Iran, S&P, Nasdaq, tariffs, etc.) + posting-day event study
  • Forward returns: +1 day, +1 week (+5 trading days), +1 month (+21 trading days) from each event date
  • Featured stories: 12 hand-curated events with headlines for portfolio narrative (see powerbi/featured_events.csv)

Technology stack

Python

Data cleaning, VADER sentiment, yfinance market pulls, event study, DuckDB star schema, CSV catalog for Power BI

Power BI

Interactive report — price context, event timelines, in-office comparisons, conditional formatting on returns

SQL

Star schema (Fact_Posts, Fact_Market, Dim_Date) with window functions and correlation queries

Cursor + Claude

AI-assisted development environment — pipeline design, DAX debugging, and this website

This site

Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — deployed on Vercel. Charts exported from Power BI PDF.

GitHub

Full reproducible repository — scripts, data catalog, Power BI files, and website source

Methodology

  • Sentiment scored before market joins to avoid look-ahead bias
  • Aggregate daily sentiment ≈ no correlation with returns — signal lives in specific events
  • All averages include every tagged event, not cherry-picked winners
  • Associations reported honestly; external factors (war, Fed, earnings) always present
